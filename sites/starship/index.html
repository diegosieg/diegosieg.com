About

The Starship Foundation is a social-profit organisation that raises funds so Starship Children's Health can better care for its young patients. On the 21st November we will be holding a black tie event at Ponsonby Central to raise funds for the Starship Foundation and in particular NICU (newborn intensive care unit). We have lofty goals and hope to raise over $100,000 on the night. John Hawkesby has very kindly given up his time to MC the event with celebrity chefs Tony Price and Chelsea Winter preparing a delicious 4 course meal.

There is only 12 tables (for 10 people) available and a table costs $2,250. Ticket price includes all food, beverages and entertainment.

To purchase a table please call Tod on 021336133.

Dr Simon Rowley MBChB, FRACP(Paed)

Simon is a leading neonatal paediatrician at National Women’s Health in Auckland Hospital, as well as working with children of all ages in private practice in Auckland. He is a trustee of Brainwave Trust Aotearoa NZ. Working within the Neonatal intensive care unit (NICU) he is often involved with premature babies and will often sit down with parents to discuss treatment for their babies. One of his responsibilities is to look after the babies born to HIV+ mothers as well as being one of the neonatologists who cares for infants born to drug addicted mothers and who may be showing signs of drug withdrawal. His particular interest is the neurobiology of infant brain development, and developmental outcomes after preterm birth and he has given talks for the Brainwave Trust to various organisations about the effect of experience on infant brain development.
